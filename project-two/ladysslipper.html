Pink Lady's Slipper

Cypripedium acaule

Pink Lady's Slipper is one of most easily recognizable wildflowers in the Valley, due in large part to its bright color and distinctive shape, which it owes to its higher classification as an orchid. It grows in clusters, flowering from April to July.

Viewing Note

Pink Lady's Slipper can be elusive, even for the experienced seeker. It only blooms in May in specific spots. Try the Tulip Poplar Trail in Shenandoah River State Park, or the Buck Ridge Trail in Shenandoah National Park.
