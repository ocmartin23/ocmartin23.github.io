Virginia Bluebells

Mertensia virginica

Virginia Bluebells have pink and purple buds and bloom blue, and are closely related to a European species that was once believed to treat lung disease. The genus Mertensia is named after the German botanist Frank Karl Mertens (1764-1831).

Viewing Note

Virginia Bluebells show different colors in different stages of blooming. In the Valley they typically bloom blue, but some flowers bloom pink or purple. Some guidebooks, like Audubon, sort this species as pink rather than blue.
